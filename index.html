About AeroShield

A practical malaria prevention solution

AeroShield is designed as a portable indoor mosquito-repellent device that releases safe vapour indoors without depending heavily on stable electricity.

🌿 Natural Repellent

Uses safe extracts like neem or lemongrass.

🔋 Low Power

Can run with a rechargeable battery or power bank.

🏠 Indoor Protection

Targets mosquito exposure indoors at night.

♻ Reusable

Designed for repeated everyday use.

Problem

Why this matters

Malaria remains a serious problem, especially for students and low-income households. Many current prevention methods are either uncomfortable, toxic, expensive, or dependent on stable electricity.

🌍 High Burden

Nigeria faces a heavy malaria burden and urgent prevention needs.

🏠 Indoor Exposure

Most malaria transmission happens indoors at night.

📚 Student Impact

Malaria disrupts learning, attendance, and productivity.

💸 Financial Pressure

Prevention and treatment costs put pressure on families.

Gap in Current Solutions

Why existing options are not enough

🕸 Mosquito Nets

Often uncomfortable and not used consistently.

🧴 Sprays

Can be toxic and expensive over time.

🔌 Electric Repellents

Depend on stable electricity supply.

🌀 Coils

May produce harmful smoke indoors.

Solution

How AeroShield works

AeroShield uses a simple indoor protection mechanism built around affordability and practicality.

1

Natural Extract

Neem or lemongrass extract is loaded into a cartridge.

2

Gradual Release

The system diffuses vapour steadily into the room.

3

Low Power Supply

Powered by rechargeable battery or power bank.

4

Continuous Defence

Provides ongoing indoor mosquito protection.

Real Life Application

Student hostel scenario

AeroShield is designed for practical day-to-day use in hostels, homes, and rooms with limited resources.

Built for real environments

A student in a hostel with no stable electricity and limited budget can use AeroShield to stay protected from mosquitoes at night.

This makes the solution more realistic, accessible, and useful for the people most affected by malaria exposure.
